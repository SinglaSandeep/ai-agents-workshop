# Hazmat Handling SOP

This SOP covers identification, storage, spill response, and disposal of
hazardous materials sold or stored at Zava stores.

## Covered materials
- Solvent-based paints, primers, stains.
- Paint thinner, mineral spirits, acetone.
- Construction adhesives, PVC primer and cement.
- Lithium-ion power-tool batteries.
- Pesticides, herbicides, and fertilizers in the garden category.
- Compressed gases (propane exchange, MAPP gas).

## Storage
- All flammables remain in the locked flammables cabinet when not on the
  sales floor.
- Pesticides and herbicides are stored in the garden lockup, separated
  from food-adjacent products (pet food, ice melt edible to wildlife).
- Lithium-ion battery returns are stored in the LiPo bag at the service
  desk, **not** the regular returns cart.
- Propane exchange cages live outside the building, behind the lumber gate.

## Spill response
1. Evacuate customers from the immediate aisle.
2. Identify the material from its SDS — SDS binders are at the service
   desk and in receiving.
3. Use the matching spill kit:
   - Universal spill kit: water-based paints, garden chemicals.
   - Flammables spill kit: solvent-based paints, thinners, adhesives.
4. Notify the shift lead immediately. Spills over 1 gallon require a call
   to the district safety hotline.
5. Bag waste in the labeled hazmat bag; do **not** put in regular trash.
6. Log incident in SafetyPro within 1 hour.

## Customer disposal questions
Zava stores do **not** accept customer drop-off of paint, batteries, or
chemicals for disposal. Direct customers to the King County Hazardous
Waste program or the manufacturer's takeback program. The exception is
lithium-ion power-tool batteries from Zava-sold brands, which the service
desk accepts under our manufacturer takeback partnership.

## Annual training
All associates complete the 30-minute hazmat e-learning module each
January. Store managers verify completion in the People Hub compliance
dashboard.
