# Zava Safety Handbook

Safety is the first responsibility of every Zava associate. This handbook
applies to all stores and is reviewed every six months.

## Personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Safety glasses are required whenever cutting, drilling, sanding, or mixing
  paint.
- Cut-resistant gloves are required at the lumber saw, fence-cut station,
  and any time handling sheet metal.
- Closed-toe slip-resistant footwear is required on the sales floor and in
  receiving.
- Hearing protection is available at the lumber yard and required when
  using the panel saw for more than 10 minutes.

## Lifting & material handling
- Two-person lift for anything over 50 lb or longer than 8 ft.
- Always use the mechanical lift assist for sheet goods (plywood, OSB,
  drywall).
- Forklift operation requires current certification on file with the store
  manager.

## Chemical & flammables
- Solvent-based paints, stains, and adhesives are stored in the flammables
  cabinet. The cabinet must be closed at all times when unattended.
- Spill kits are located at: paint counter, plumbing aisle, and receiving
  dock. See the hazmat-handling SOP for spill procedure.
- Never mix bleach and ammonia products on the same return cart.

## Customer & ladder safety
- Customers may not climb store ladders — direct them to the orange "Ask an
  Associate" call buttons.
- Spotter required for ladder use above 8 ft.
- Block off aisles with the yellow "Wet Floor" cones whenever floors are
  being mopped or stocked from a lift.

## Incident reporting
All injuries, near-misses, and customer incidents must be reported to the
shift lead within 15 minutes and logged in SafetyPro before end of shift.
The store manager files OSHA-recordable events within 24 hours.
