# Q4 2025 Marketing Wrap — Cross-Campaign Notes

A short cross-campaign retrospective covering the Holiday Hardware Gift
Guide, Winter Storm Prep, and Fall Garden Cleanup campaigns.

## Channel-mix observations
- **Email open rates** chain-wide averaged 22.4% in Q4, down from 24.8%
  in Q3. We attribute this to list fatigue from the gift-guide cadence.
  2026 recommendation: cap holiday-window sends at 6 per subscriber.
- **YouTube pre-roll** continued to outperform Instagram for power-tool
  campaigns. Plan to allocate more 2026 budget there.
- **Pinterest** is now the strongest channel for the paint category.
  Brief the creative team for 2026 paint campaigns accordingly.

## Inventory & operations
The **FC-to-store overnight restock** workflow remained the limiting
factor in several promo windows. Specifically, weekend velocity at the
Seattle store on paint SKUs and at Bellevue on cordless power-tool kits
both ran ahead of replenishment two campaigns in a row.

Recommended 2026 ops change: **pre-position 1.4× the modeled weekend
demand** at flagship stores for any promo featuring a doorbuster bundle.

## Store-level highlights
- Seattle drove 24% of chain revenue in Q4 — the flagship effect is
  intact.
- Spokane Winter Storm Prep over-indexed on generators by 38%.
- Tacoma's holiday hardware revenue was flat YoY — investigate creative
  fit for the Tacoma demographic in 2026.

## Carry-forwards to 2026
1. Cap holiday email cadence at 6 sends per subscriber.
2. Pre-position weekend inventory at Seattle and Bellevue for any
   doorbuster promo.
3. Shift more paint budget to Pinterest.
4. Re-evaluate Tacoma holiday creative.
