# Spring Paint Sale 2025 — Post-Mortem

## Headline result
- Paint-category revenue: **+12% YoY** vs. a +18% target.
- ROAS: **1.42×** (target 1.5×).
- Email open rate 23.1%, click-through 3.8% — both slightly under target.

## What worked
- The "2-for-$60" Premium Interior Eggshell bundle drove 38% of all paint
  units sold during the window. This is the strongest performing promo
  mechanic in three years of paint campaigns.
- Bellevue and Tacoma exceeded their per-store paint revenue targets by
  +5% and +9% respectively.
- Pinterest creative outperformed Instagram by 2× on cost-per-engagement.
  Recommend shifting budget for 2026.

## What didn't work — and why we missed plan

### Seattle paint stock-outs (root cause of the YoY miss)
**`ZV-PNT-001` (Premium Interior Eggshell) at the Seattle store ran out
twice** during the campaign:
- Weekend 1 (April 5–6): out for 36 hours.
- Weekend 3 (April 19–20): out for 22 hours.

The Seattle store entered the campaign with **48 units** of `ZV-PNT-001`
on hand. The campaign's 2-for-$60 mechanic drove an unexpectedly high
attach rate; weekend velocity was ~25 units/day vs. the 12/day forecast.
Online FC overnight restock was triggered both weekends but lag time cost
roughly 60 units of foregone sales — equivalent to ~$1,800 in
paint-category revenue and a measurable knock-on to brush / roller
attach.

### Mitigation for 2026
1. **Pre-position 200+ units of `ZV-PNT-001`** at the Seattle store
   before launch.
2. Increase reorder threshold for paint SKUs at Seattle from 15 to 25
   during April–May.
3. Auto-trigger overnight FC restock if Seattle paint inventory drops
   below 30 units, rather than waiting on a manager ticket.

### Other minor misses
- Tinter-counter wait times at Seattle exceeded 25 minutes on the launch
  weekend. Mitigation: schedule two associates on the mixer Fri–Sun.
- Earth Day mid-campaign refresh creative underperformed. The eco-paint
  angle didn't land — consumers responded better to project-completion
  messaging. Adjust 2026 mid-campaign creative.

## Discount-mechanic note
Both stores escalated several customer requests for additional discounts
on tinted paint after the customer changed their mind on color. Per
chain policy, tinted paint is non-returnable; managers used the
discount-approval matrix to issue 10–15% goodwill credits where the
customer was a Zava Pro member, but this is judgment-based. Recommend
clarifying the goodwill-credit guidance in the 2026 store-manager
briefing.
